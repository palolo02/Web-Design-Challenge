3.- Social and Economic factors shown a correlation inverse but p_value show that this is not a strong correlation.

Based on the correlation result between Income and Crime, we can say that there is an inverse relationship between these two variables and Crime. It means when the % Income Increases or % Graduates increases our % Offence per person Decreases (meaning there is a definite, consequential relationship between these two variables). However, our Income and Graduates P- value are 0.17 and 0.82 respectively. It means Our p-value are greater than 0.05 significance level, so we fail to reject the null hypothesis, it indicates that our samples did not provide sufficient evidence to conclude that the effect exists. In addition to that we observed that the size or the sample plays a big role to test a hypothesis, here we got a small data sample to work with.
