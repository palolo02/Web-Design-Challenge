Data Analysis

As every dataset hides a lot of ways to tell what is happening, the gun data file is no exception. From the beginning we observed that there are general patterns were we can start making assumptions of what is happening, how the data is related to itself, how we can see it from another perspective and lastly, how we can learn from it. Let's have a look at what we have

Correlation between the # of licenses and gun crimes

On this graph we can appreciate that the countries with the largest number of licenses are not necessarily the most violent. This is a consequence of stronger gun regulation laws like in New York and California that also are the most populated states.

After considering the deadliest type of incidents, we wanted to look at the same relationshp but this time by only cosidering the numer of injured we can find in those moments. Unsurprisingly, we noticed a bigger distribution in armed robberies. A reason that may explain this behavior could be the purpose. According to some information, during robberies, perpetrators only want to get away this other's belongins, not actually kille them (unless they do not have any other option), which is contrary to what we can find in close relationships.

Close-relationships Incidents are deadlier than those in which the victim(s) is/are unknown (like robberies).

In order to answer this question, we need to focus on the key types of crime that ocurr more often. Once we stablish that group, we then compare the distribution of each one incidents according to the number of their victims they got (% lethality) and plot their behaviour. If we concentrate in the first three categories (tallest curves), we see that those are actually close-relationships, whereas the shortest correspond to categories in which the victims could potentially not know the perpetrator. We are assumming that in a robbery, typically the victm is randomly chosen, otherwise it will be more likely that they end up in jail. The take-away from this plot is that those conflicts that involve close-ones are more likely to have more victims.

Southern states (near Mexico) present higher deaths than other locations in US

Here, we observe where those incidents happened in the US. At first sight, there is concentration of violence in south east states, a zone that is not really close to mexican border. As we are thinking that souternwest states have more levels of violence, let's split the incidents in those fours cuadrants and see the results.

The results show something different from what we expected. According to the graph, the deadliest zone in three years has been southeast and in second place comes northeast. Referring to our hypothesis, we can say that the closer we get to Mexico does not imply a hiher level of deaths in those states. At this point, we can remember that states such as District of Columbia or Lussiana are among the top 5 more violent states and they are situated in the southeast cuadrant, making more sense of the data.
